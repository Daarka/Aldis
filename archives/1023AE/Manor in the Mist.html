The Tapestry's Reflection

The tapestry on the wall to your right matches the mirror in its dimensions, and accounts for almost the entirety of the wall's surface. It's gorgeous and clearly very finely crafted. You can easily see that the tapestry tells a story, starting towards the side nearest the front door, and progressing as it reaches down the hall, so that the narrative seems to follow you as you walk through the parlor. That narrative looks to be the story of the Skhalhammer family in tableaux, beginning with their origins as traitors to their kingdom, continuing through their work as mercenaries and cutthroats followed by their time as privateers for the crown and culminating with Jhaal assuming power and leveraging his family's wealth to create one of the most powerful corporations in the world. It's all presented very regally, with the Skhalhammers depicted dressed in royal blues and gold, and even the illustrations of their traitorous origins laid out in a way that seems to paint them in a very noble light. When you look at the tapestry's reflection in the mirror, however, none of these illustrations are present. But it tells a story nonetheless, in the same tableaux style. As your eyes follow the slightly gloomier-looking weavings, its depicted narrative opens with woven illustrations of beautiful lands and buildings, like nothing any of you have seen before. Though the medium of a tapestry has limitations, you can tell that the images it means to convey are of something truly breathtaking. The land looks somewhat tropical, dense with flora and fauna of the most colorful variety--and the buildings depicted do not interrupt that color. Instead, they only amplify it, with much of the surfaces decorated in brilliant colors woven into the architecture. You realize the tapestry is depicting a stunning, vibrant excess of stained glass making up gorgeous accents across these structures. And there are people depicted in these scenes, too. People dressed in styles also not recognized by you, but equally vibrant and exotic and colorful as their surroundings. But the scenes evolve as your eyes travel along the timeline the tapestry has laid out. A figure stands, starkly out of place in the beautiful setting, seemingly exploring it and its people. The colorful people seem to show no aggression, but there are illustrations showing them holding their hands up to the stranger, barring him entry from certain places. One even gestures for the stranger to return to a ship seen off the coast. The stranger is shown retreating, obligingly withdrawing from the colorful civilization. But not to the ship; only to the beach where he is alone. An arcane circle on the ground heralds the arrival of more strangers. These men are in uniform, dressed in blues and golds. These men carry weapons. The illustration rapidly devolves into the armed men storming the beautiful structures. They brute-force their way past the people's gentle boundaries. Where the weave of the tapestry illustrates the colorful people who have been cut down in the foreigner's wake, there is no red thread; the fabric of the tapestry itself is sliced over the images of their bodies, and a slow trickle of crimson stains the fabric down to the floor, where it pools on the hardwood. The strangers enter the place the colorful people wanted to keep them out of. There is illustrated a small but beautiful box, like a cube of radiant stained glasswork. The backdrop of the beautiful, vibrant land and buildings is no longer present as the illustrations continue. The box is still colorful and beautiful, but its surroundings are dull in comparison, where it sits on a gilded pedestal. Hovering over it is a dwarven man, dressed immaculately in golds and blues. The glow of the box reflects in his eyes, hands reaching for it, coveting it despite already possessing it. There is a great splintering of color next. The fabric of the tapestry bears another slice marring through it, though this one does not bleed. Instead, like a tear through reality, a horrid monster bursts into the weaving. The last illustration the reflected tapestry depicts is that of the Skhalhammer Manor. Encircling the building like a great serpent constricting its prey is an entity that horrifies you to look upon--yet no matter how you squint at it, the details slip away from your mind. Only the emotion remains: a feeling of dread in your core.

Ozymanthios

I have given you the fury of nature. I have given you the power of ancients. What have you done to deserve this boon? My prodigy sees his reflection and grimaces. My prodigy drowns his life in amnesia and strangles his future in distractions, lest he sit in stillness and know the depths to which he hates himself. Hates MY divine creation. I am not simply slain; the only ancient power that has been slain is that which I blessed you with, and you are the Great Hunter who felled that beast. Have I not been beside you every breath of your life? Have I not sang fury into your veins, spurred you to find the strength I gave unto you? What more must I do to drive he whom is wreathed in my greatness to raise his chin and make bloody conquest of the demons who haunt him? What more must I do before the prince of the thorns knows he does not draw breath by accident? You seek to rid yourself of that which abrades your precious flesh. And so you flee it. The faster you run, the hotter the excitement burns in the frothing teeth of that which pursues. Your weakness does naught but arouse the predators that snap at your heels and lay waste to your sanity. You cannot hide from what hungers for your blood. You cannot run faster than it. You must conquer. You must burn hotter. You will match my righteous fury, or you will become consumed in it. If you heed my call, you need never flee again. If you answer the writhing in your blood, you will sunder all who dare threaten rise against you in thorn and holy flame. Your back will hold straight and your head held high, and you will know your blood is of the divine ancients. What would linger for you to fear then, Princeling? You are well equipped to find your own soft comforts. I am the power in your wrath. I am the storm. I am the earthquake. My fury does not discriminate. I have no mercy. I am the force of nature. But you, Yarrow, have the unique chance to rise in my image. I make no mistakes with my blessings. I know not discouragement--even against your adamant avoidance. But even a worm will turn, princeling. And I shall continue to provoke you until you grow tired of running, and instead, turn and face me. There is a story you think you know. Let me show you how it was truly written. Slay me, Yarrow. If I am the subject of your sleepless nights, then put a sword in my heart and reclaim your peace. Forge a weapon from the wrath you have kept dormant. Fell me and seize your crown, Prince of Thorns, or I will haunt your dreams for the rest of your life, goring every scrap of peace you try to hide behind until you do.

As the body of the boar falls beneath you, you watch as his hulking form seems to wither and crumple, the tangle of brambles that almost seemed to make up his anatomy now coming unraveled. The mass crumbles into itself, shrinking, soon no longer even resembling a boar at all--until finally, the shifting brambles still. There, at the center, what had been the very core of Ozymanthios--perhaps where a natural boar's heart would have been--a strange bloom has taken root, splitting up from the salt-soaked planks. It's no flower, but... it has a strange, alien beauty to it all the same. Even as it is covered in nasty thorns, some even protruding like large, thick fangs from its tangled stems. Something about the little knot of vines not only strikes you as beautiful, but as sacred. The vibrant green is accented by shocks of vermillion, speckles on the jagged leaves resembling splatterings of viscera. There is a sense of thrumming vitality from it, the tips of its leaves and vines stirring gently, like idle, curious tentacles. The vines are drawn to your arm. With them comes not the bite of countless thorns--but a strange surge of strength. Of warmth. You watch as your own flesh seems to move in reply, the faint shapes of vines that press together to make up your body shifting, adjusting. Making room. The newer vines crawl through your skin. It's not comfortable, but it feels right in a way you never would have expected. It surges vitality through you, even as it visibly seems to split your flesh and burrow living brambles into your body. But the agony that would bring anyone else does not come to you. You feel battered, bruised, chewed up and spit out in just about every possible way. But as you stand over what was once a boar from your nightmares, and is now merely a mound of thorny vines, it is impossible to deny the way the conquest rings through you. You are Fucked Up... but you did something here. It's not the end of your troubles. But maybe... it's a start. As you revel in that alien sensation, the brambles that entangled your allies loosen, then begin to wither and fall away. The rest of you undoubtedly earned several superficial cuts and pricks throughout the experience you were forced to bear witness to--but are otherwise unharmed, and no longer restrained.
