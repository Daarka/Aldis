Niamh hadnt known what to expect in the days following her evening stroll with Cha Cha. Ilya. The novelty still hadn't worn off, and she was enjoying using it behind closed doors. For all the ways that their situation had changed though, in many ways it really... Hadn't. At least with current circumstances being the way they were, it was hard to really pivot when the days were filled with prep for the long voyage looming before them and left little room for anything else. The "coats" she commissioned had been picked up and subsequently dropped off for their enchantments, the pemmican prep had been almost entirely completed, and Cain and Cha Cha had really started to get the hang of the sailing knots. She did, however, intend to keep to their agreement that they'd steal one more evening out in the town enjoying the peace and bluster of the coming holiday season, and that would come. Tonight though, she intended to keep another promise - one that she'd warned him about that morning when he'd left for his errands and she'd set up a small assembly line on the floor of the kitchen. Now, at the close of the day, she sat in one of the kitchen chairs and used her teeth to cut the last of the heavy thread she'd been using to stitch leather together. Sparing the build in her hands a few moments just to give it a visual once-over and check the air flow once it was pressed against her face, she set the mask down on the table beside the others. Five simple full-face masks of dark, heavy leather, each one fitted with rounds of tinted glass over the eyes and an aperture over the mouth that jutted out like a short muzzle. And on the end, a much older version that had clearly been the blueprint - leather so dark it looked black, shiny with age and wear, the mouthpiece built upon and carved into until it resembled a snarling maw filled with rows of cruel looking fangs. The thick red glass covering the eyes had been cut into glowering almond shapes and set into their sockets under a sculpted brow. The entirety of the surface was covered in a filigree pattern - imperfect and messy in some places but clearly cut by the hand of its bearer. Old, worn, and with a light veneer of dust from being hidden away for years. Where it belongs, she thought with a grimace, sitting back in the chair as her gaze fell on the familiar features. Her hand drifted to the bottle of rum she'd dug out somewhere between the third and fourth masks, refilling the glass sitting before her, but setting it back down beside the empty one before the seat beside her. Was it excessive to have prepared these, knowing the only risk of needing them lay in a small fleet roaming the ocean on the complete opposite coast? Probably. But she had too much to lose now to not be over-preparing.
