It had been a couple days since the party had returned from the manor in Longwatch; four, to be exact, with about a day having passed since Jaska had been cooking breakfast for the group in Yarrow's apartment. Enough things needed to be in order before the group of idiots could catapult themselves into uncharted waters, and so there were at least a few days of preparation to be had before leaving Fielbarry. Kelnari was among those who had the least to prepare, which left him in a strange place that felt like floating amid a dull buzz of chaos. He had... so many thoughts bouncing around in his skull, strings plucked from every corner of the emotional spectrum. It had left him a bit dissociative, seeming almost shut down much of the time. How much of that had to do with what lay ahead versus what lay dead and yet undiscovered in a nearby forest was uncertain. Still in that strange fugue-like fog, he wasn't particularly sure where he was going until he found himself stepping over the threshold of the Creeping Root Inn. He paused for a moment in the doorway, very much as if trying to remember why he came into a room, before slipping out of the walkway so he wasn't blocking traffic. An involuntary glance was given towards the bizarre, arachnid-flavored simic hybrid settled comfortably near the door--whose singular eye regarded him in return with a matching lack of facial expression--before Kel politely reined himself in from staring, shuffling towards the bar. He still wasn't sure why his feet had taken him here, but... maybe getting some good landlocked food in his system would make the voyage ahead easier. Maybe it would delay the occult hunger pains that would inevitably creep in at some point. What kind of food did he even like again? It took him a second to remember as he dazedly slipped onto a barstool. His gaze wandered until it found a menu, skimming over it once. Then again, because he hadn't actually succeeded in reading anything. Third time was the charm. It wouldn't be hard to notice how distracted he was. Enough so that he didn't seem to take any notice of more familiar faces around him, off in his own little convoluted and tangled mess of a world.
