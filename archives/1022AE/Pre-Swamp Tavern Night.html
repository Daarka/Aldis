It was late afternoon in Fielbarry, with the very beginnings of the evening crowd starting to show up at the Creeping Root Inn for dinner, drinks, and idle socializing. Valentine was in her usual place behind the bar; a tiefling woman of average height and curvy build, with a wild mane of beautiful hair that grew in a stunning gradient from soft pink-violet to warm orange and butter yellow, looking like a sunrise sky that gorgeously complimented her soft lavender complexion. As was typical for many tieflings, her eyes lacked pupils, and yet something about their pastel yellow color looked oddly glazed over; suspicion of this being the case would only be further encouraged by watching her navigate her space. Though she did so with amazing fluidity and familiarity, it wasn't terribly hard to see that her body language was that of someone with a significant vision impairment, potentially even total blindness. But that scarcely seemed to stop her. Her cheerful, sunshiney disposition was warmly cast on any patron she saw to, and everyone seemed to know her by name. That included a very petite girl who greeted her softly as she helped herself up onto a barstool where there were two empty ones side by side. Whereas the barkeep was, fairly objectively, stunning by any beauty standard, this girl was... not. She was waifish and quite sickly looking, with gaunt, colorless skin and equally pale, colorless hair that was fine, straight, and cut to shoulder-length. Most striking--apart from the fact that her flesh looked paper-thin and distinctly unhealthy--were her eyes; inky black sclera and honey gold irises, with an unnaturally faint pupil at their center. She was dressed in neutral, unassuming colors and modest styles, in a long sleeved dress with leggings on underneath that kept most of her body concealed, the extra layers of fabric doing a meager amount to disguise how unusually narrow and frail her actual body was. But her ears were not pointed, and she had no horns, tail, or anything that confidently identified her race at all. Just a strange looking little creature, who politely asked for a glass of milk and seemed content to mind her own business at the bar.
