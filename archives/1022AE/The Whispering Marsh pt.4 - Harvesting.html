As the aboleth broodmother collapsed to the ground, the numerous, boneless tentacles limply sliding down across each other in a mess of flesh, ink, and unnatural blood, the battle came to a close. After a brief pause to take a collective exhale, the party spurred into movement again; time could not be wasted in these critical moments, or else the bounty of valuable components in front of them would go to waste. Reflexively, Cassiana cast Gentle Repose on the newly-created corpse, greatly slowing the process of magical deterioration to buy them more wiggle room. Kaskra and Cairn, previously agreed upon by the party to helm the harvesting endeavor, fell into step with one another, as Cass and Clove bestowed quick boons upon each of them to aid in their tasks. Kaskra was confident in his familiarity with such specimens, and knowledgably guided Cairn through the process of carving open the broodmother, carefully extracting the pieces of its otherworldly anatomy that they found value in. As each component was cleanly carved free, Achoris, Niamh, and Clove assisted in stabilizing it for transport back to town under Kaskra's guidance, ensuring it would not lose its potency--and value--on the journey. Achoris, somehow, was able to summon a spectral chest, a container of two by three cubic feet in capacity--though (potentially) already housing a few items. Working together in this manner, pieces were extracted--with Cairn managing to avoid cutting into anything hazardous in the process--carefully preserved through magical means, and then stowed away in Achoris' chest, which he explained could be summoned again once they were back in town. Even the broodmother's essence, an ethereal and intangible pulse of aether through her body that threatened to weaken and dissipate in the absence of her life, was expertly captured, harnessed, and contained in a waiting aether crystal meant to house just such an energy. As that mote of magic, almost akin to electricity in nature, transferred into the crystal, its rosy hue bled out to be replaced with a dusky teal color, emitting a faint glow of the same appearance. When the most valuable and time-sensitive components had been seen to, the party's attention shifted to the more mundane components, as the last remnants of aether that had woven through the broodmother dissipated back into the world. With the time constraint no longer looming over them, they were free to carve away at their leisure, no longer requiring the assistance of everyone simultaneously. By the time this was all concluded, it was noted they had about ten minutes left to make use of their ability to walk on water, or at least before it would have to be re-cast. But for the moment, they could move about the space without being submerged, exploring the broodmother's lair. After all... they still hadn't found an aether node. Endeavors to harvest mundane items from the aboleth must roll a Dexterity check.
